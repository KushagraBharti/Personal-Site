Values, Beliefs, Writings, Thoughts, and Predictions

01 epistemic velocity

Category: value

Summary: Lifelong learning as epistemic hygiene: curiosity, discipline, and pressure against stale competence.

Epistemic Velocity

I treat learning as upkeep, not ambition.

The half-life of useful knowledge keeps shrinking. Competence calcifies. You stop noticing the terrain moved because the patch you're standing on still feels solid.

What I'm watching in myself:

  • how fast I can re-derive something I forgot
  • whether I update without making it identity-shaped
  • whether the breadth compounds or just collects
  • whether I'm getting enough adversarial input to expose bad priors

Curiosity is cheap. Discipline is what stops it becoming drift.

You don't usually get out-thought. You get out-iterated.

02 adaptive agency

Category: belief

Summary: High-agency adaptation under ambiguity: fast revision, clear authorship, tools kept subordinate.

Adaptive Agency

I care more about reaction time than position.

Most plans rot on contact. The skill is re-deriving fast, without making a thing of it. You drop the version of yourself that was right yesterday because that version is now a liability.

How I try to operate:

  • think from primitives, not analogies to last quarter
  • move before certainty turns into procrastination
  • change direction without performing the change
  • treat AI as exoskeleton, not identity

When intelligence becomes common infrastructure, the floor on answers drops. The premium relocates: context, taste, conviction, execution.

A tool that absorbs your agency is just a tool that's using you.

03 aesthetic judgment

Category: thought

Summary: Taste as compressed perception: rhythm, restraint, framing, and selection when generation is cheap.

Aesthetic Judgment

Film taught me taste before software did.

A cut changes causality. A frame changes what matters. Silence outranks explanation more often than not. Two shots adjacent to each other can carry a thought neither shot owns alone.

Product is the same instrument:

  • what gets foregrounded
  • what disappears
  • where the rhythm slips
  • what's technically correct and still wrong

Taste is compressed perception. The work is choosing what to leave out.

When generation gets cheap, judgment gets expensive.

I want work that feels authored, not assembled.

04 ambient agency

Category: prediction

Summary: Software as ambient infrastructure: fewer destinations, stronger memory, cleaner preservation of intent.

Ambient Agency

The software I want stops asking me to be its integration layer.

Most tools force you to rebuild the same scaffolding every time. Reopen the thread. Locate the file. Reconstruct yesterday's mental state. Translate your goal into the tool's grammar before the tool can help. The friction is the product.

A better substrate would:

  • carry memory across sessions
  • carry context across surfaces
  • delegate with verification, not abdication
  • automate without erasing legibility
  • surface itself only when judgment is required

That's why AI tools, agents, planning systems, and artifact workflows interest me. The demo is bait. The substrate is the point.

You shouldn't have to be the connective tissue between your own apps.

Fewer destinations. More continuity.
