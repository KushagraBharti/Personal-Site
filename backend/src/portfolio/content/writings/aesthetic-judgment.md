# Aesthetic Judgment

Film taught me taste before software did.

A cut changes causality. A frame changes what matters. Silence outranks explanation more often than not. Two shots adjacent to each other can carry a thought neither shot owns alone.

Product is the same instrument:

- what gets foregrounded
- what disappears
- where the rhythm slips
- what's technically correct and still wrong

Taste is compressed perception. The work is choosing what to leave out.

> When generation gets cheap, judgment gets expensive.

I want work that feels authored, not assembled.
